Research

I develop multiscale theoretical tools to explore the intricate relationships between crystallography, microstructures, and material properties in battery materials.

Crystallographic design

I develop mathematical models, across multiple length scales, to understand fundamental material behavior. Specifically, I am interested in developing theoretical and computational tools that predict material response to various loading conditions in phase transformation materials. These theoretical tools will guide the discovery of materials with enhanced capabilities, and provide a systematic methodology to engineer materials from a bottom-up approach. Methods of interest to our group include, phase-field models, cohesive-zone models, molecular dynamics, and phase-field crystal models.
